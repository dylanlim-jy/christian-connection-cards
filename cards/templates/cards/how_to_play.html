How to Play

2 players 3 - 6 players

Step 1

Questions are divided into 3 stages:

  • Stage 1 'Perception': on self-image and how others perceive you.
  • Stage 2 'Connection': personal questions on your past, present, and eternal future.
  • Stage 3 'Edification': questions to help build one another up to the image of Christ.

  • You may choose how many questions per stage you want to play (minimum 3).

Step 2

Youngest player takes the first turn.
Read out the first question to the other player who will respond.
The active player is allowed to ask follow-up questions, but the responding player can decline to answer.

Step 3

Swap players; the responding player is now the active player and will be the one asking the question.
As both players progress through the stages, the questions are meant to become more personal.

Step 4

At the end of the game, each player writes (or texts) a personal note to the other player.
They are not allowed to read the note right away - read it once you have split up / left the room.

Step 1

Questions are divided into 3 stages:

  • Stage 1 'Perception': on self-image and how others perceive you.
  • Stage 2 'Connection': personal questions on your past, present, and eternal future.
  • Stage 3 'Edification': questions to help build one another up to the image of Christ.

  • You may choose how many questions per stage you want to play (minimum 3).

Step 2

Every player will some time writing down 3 impressions they have of every other player.
Don't tell each other anything about this yet!

Step 3

The turn order starts from the youngest player to the oldest player.
The first player will read out a question and every other player will respond.
The active player is allowed to ask follow-up questions, but other players can decline to answer.

Step 4

Once the active player's turn is up, the turn goes to the second player, who reads the next question displayed.
Once the question is read, every other player will respond. As all players progress through the stages, the questions are meant to become more personal.

Step 5

At the end of the game, each player shares the first impression of other players that they wrote earlier.
After reading out the first impressions, players are also encouraged to share how that impression has changed over the course of this game.
