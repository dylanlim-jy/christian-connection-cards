How to Play

2 players 3 - 6 players

Step 1

Questions are divided into 3 stages:

  • Stage 1 'Perception' questions are about your self-image and how others view you.
  • Stage 2 'Connection' questions are deeper, more personal questions about your past, present, and eternal future.
  • Stage 3 'Edification' questions are questions that help us build one another up to the image of Christ.

  • You may choose how many questions per stage you want to play (minimum 3, default 5).

Step 2

The youngest player starts the game first, as Player 1.
Player 1 will read out the first question displayed to Player 2, who will respond.
Player 1 is allowed to ask follow-up questions, but Player 2 is not obligated to answer.

Step 3

Once Player 1's turn is up, Player 2 moves to the next question displayed and gets Player 1 to respond.
As both players progress through the stages, the questions will become more personal.

Step 4

At the end of the game, each player writes (or texts) a personal note to the other player.
They are not allowed to read the note right away - read it once you have split up / left the room.

Step 1

Questions are divided into 3 stages:

  • Stage 1 'Perception' questions are about your self-image and how others view you.
  • Stage 2 'Connection' questions are deeper, more personal questions about your past, present, and eternal future.
  • Stage 3 'Edification' questions are questions that help us build one another up to the image of Christ.

  • You may choose how many questions per stage you want to play (minimum 3, default 5).

Step 2

Every player will some time writing down 3 impressions they have of every other player.

Step 3

The turn order starts from the youngest player to the oldest player.
The first player will read out the first question displayed and every other player will respond.
The active player is allowed to ask follow-up questions, but other players are not obligated to answer.

Step 4

Once the active player's turn is up, the turn goes to the second player, who reads the next question displayed.
Once the question is read, every other player will respond. As all players progress through the stages, the questions will become more personal.

Step 5

At the end of the game, each player reads their first impressions of other players.
After reading out the first impressions, players are also encouraged to share how that impression has changed over the course of this game.
